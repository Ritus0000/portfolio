What it is

Not a scraper. Not a chatbot wrapper. A decision system.

PRACA2 turns a vague search direction into inspectable evidence, candidate-aware review, application preparation and response tracking. Its core engine, MarketForge, searches around the actual mechanics of the work: synthetic visual data, 3D simulation, rendering pipelines, digital twins, technical art and spatial AI product work.

Search intent builderTransforms a human direction like "synthetic data with Blender" into structured briefs, source preferences, query seeds, exclusions and exploration settings.
DeepDiscoveryFinds job and opportunity evidence across job boards, ATS pages, company career pages, universities, labs, startups and manual URLs.
Candidate memoryStores profile capsules, capabilities, constraints, tools and evidence so every review knows the actual candidate context.
Semantic reviewExplains fit based on work mechanics and marks uncertainty as manual-check evidence instead of inventing fake precision.
Application controlKeeps documents, status, decision notes, confirmations, replies and follow-up state attached to each vacancy.
Feedback loopUses outcomes and human decisions to improve next searches, source routing and candidate-specific filtering.

Why it matters

Classic job search breaks for interdisciplinary work.

A role can be relevant even when the title is wrong, and irrelevant even when the title looks perfect. PRACA2 focuses on similarity to real work patterns: 3D scenes, materials, camera paths, rendering automation, dataset generation, annotations, quality control and product reasoning around AI workflows.

Ranks by work-mechanics similarity, not only title or industry.
Keeps uncertainty visible through manual-check flags.
Connects search, review, documents and responses in one loop.
Supports a platform-flexible profile across Blender, Unity, Unreal, Omniverse and other simulation/rendering tools.
The product idea is simple: reduce a chaotic opportunity market into evidence that can be inspected, remembered and acted on.
